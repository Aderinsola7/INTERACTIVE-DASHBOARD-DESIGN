# INTERACTIVE-DASHBOARD-DESIGN
This project transitions complex global financial data into actionable intelligence for policymakers, analysts, and students. Working within a policy think-tank, the objective was to bridge the gap between raw economic indicators and strategic decision-making through an interactive, one-page Power BI dashboard. This tool allows non-technical stakeholders to explore two decades of economic performance across 44 indicators, specifically focusing on the period from 2001 to 2020.

The analytical core of the Global Economic Outlook Dashboard is grounded in data from the International Monetary Fund (IMF) World Economic Outlook. To provide a holistic view of national and regional health, I focused the visualization on four headline macroeconomic pillars:

- Real GDP Growth (%): To measure levels of economic activity and expansion.

- Average Consumer Price Inflation (%): To monitor price stability and purchasing power.

- Unemployment Rate (%): To assess the health and status of the global labor market.

- General Government Gross Debt (% of GDP): To evaluate the sustainability of public finances.

To move beyond isolated country data, I performed a data join with a supplemental World Bank "Country Groupings" file. This integration was critical for providing context, allowing users to compare individual nations against their specific geographic regions and income groups (e.g., High Income vs. Low Income).

The final deliverable is a highly functional, interactive interface that features:

Dynamic Filtering: Simple dropdowns for Year, Region, and Country.

Trend Analysis: Time-series visualizations to track volatility and growth over the 20-year span.

Comparative Positioning: A multivariate scatter plot to identify correlations between debt, inflation, and growth.

Composite Profiles: A distilled macroeconomic summary for rapid country-level assessment.

By simplifying the complexity of IMF datasets, this dashboard serves as a vital resource for the economic community, enabling users to identify global trends and regional outliers with just a few clicks.
